Pork Risotto

Description

A classic, slow cooking risotto recipe with salty pork and tender bell pepper slices.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp chicken bullion
  • 2 scallions
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 lemon
  • 9 oz pork sausage
  • 3/4 cup arborio rice
  • 1tbsp italian seasoning
  • 1/4 cup parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 425 degrees. In a medium pot, combine bullion and 4 cups water. Bring to a simmer and reduce to low.
  2. Thinly slice scallions, separate whites from greens. Peel and mince garlic. Halve, core and slice bell pepper 1/2 inch thick strips. Quarter lemon.
  3. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add sausage and cook, breaking meat into pieces until browned and cooked through. Turn off heat and transfer to a paper towel lined plate with a slotted spoon. Leave as much oil in pan as possible.
  4. Heat pan with reserved oil over medium heat. Add scallion whites, garlic, rice and 1/2 tsp italian seasoning. Cook, stirring until whites are softened and rice is translucent. Add 1/2 cup stock and stir.
  5. Repeat process with remaining stock adding 1/2 cup at a time. Stirring until liquid has mostly absorbed, until rice is al dente and mixture is creamy.
  6. Meanwhile, toss bell pepper on a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil and italian seasoning as well as salt and pepper. Roast until softened and lightly charred.
  7. Once risotto is done, stir in pork, bell pepper, half of the parmesan and 2 tbsp of butter. Add a squeeze of lemon juice to taste and season with salt and pepper. Serve and enjoy!
